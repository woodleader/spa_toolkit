Regulatory Marks

What This Covers

General decision support for CE, WEEE, FCC, and UN ECE R10. This page is tuned for cable assemblies, automotive electronic products, and finished non-electrical parts, and uses conservative "check the route" outputs where vehicle-specific exclusions are often fact-pattern dependent.

Use Boundaries

This helper follows general guidance commonly used in Europe for RoHS/WEEE scope, vehicle-specific exclusions, RED radio products, FCC Part 15, and UN ECE R10 automotive EMC, while also showing when a finished mechanical part is outside those electronics-focused routes.

  • Generic or dual-use products with electrical or radio functions are treated as normal EEE unless a narrower exclusion clearly applies.
  • Vehicle-specific products get a CHECK result when outcome depends on whether the product is a separately marketed EEE item or an integrated transport/vehicle part.
  • Wireless products stay on the RED/CE path even when used in vehicles.
  • Finished non-electrical parts are shown mainly to confirm when these specific marking routes do not normally apply.
  • UN ECE R10 is shown as the automotive approval route, not as a substitute for every EU product-law obligation.

The CLEPA statement adds automotive-industry interpretation at the ELV/WEEE-RoHS boundary. The Commission FAQ is older and not legally binding, so it is best used as interpretive background.

Decision Helper

Outcome wording is intentionally strict:

  • REQUIRED means the route is normally expected.
  • CHECK means the route depends on scope or exclusion facts.
  • NO means the mark is generally not expected for the described product form.
